Maintenance Tips

Practical advice on how to properly care for your windows, doors and mosquito nets — so they last long and work flawlessly.

Windows Doors Mosquito Nets

Window Care & Maintenance

Regular maintenance extends the life of your windows and saves money on repairs.

01

Clean frames twice a year

Wash PVC or aluminium frames with warm water and a mild detergent. Never use abrasives or solvents — they damage the surface and accelerate ageing.

🍂 Seasonal
02

Lubricate hardware annually

Locks, hinges and joints should be lubricated with specialist window oil or white technical petroleum jelly. Dry hardware wears out faster and can damage the frame.

🔄 Regular
03

Check seals every autumn

Before the heating season, inspect the rubber seals — they must not be hardened, cracked or loose. Worn seals are the main cause of draughts and heat loss.

🍂 Seasonal
04

Adjust windows twice a year

In spring switch to the summer setting (sash presses slightly lighter for ventilation), in autumn to the winter setting (tight closing). This reduces heat loss by up to 30%.

🍂 Seasonal
05

Clean drainage channels

The drainage grooves in the bottom profiles often get clogged with dirt and leaves. Clean them every spring — otherwise water can enter the frame and cause damp and mould.

🔄 Regular
06

Don't paint PVC frames

PVC windows are not designed to be painted — paint peels off and damages the material. If you want to change the colour, ask about professional lamination or frame replacement.

ℹ️ Good to know

Important: if your window closes unevenly against the frame at the top or bottom — that is an adjustment issue, not a replacement one. Contact us before planning a costly solution.

Door Care & Maintenance

Properly maintained doors operate silently, close securely and provide safety for decades.

01

Oil hinges once a year

Squeaky doors signal dry hinges. Apply a few drops of oil to each hinge and open/close the door several times so the oil distributes evenly.

🔄 Regular
02

Service the lock cylinder

If the key turns with resistance or the door closes with difficulty — lubricate the lock cylinder with graphite powder or a specialist aerosol. Never use WD-40 — it damages the lock mechanism.

🔄 Regular
03

Adjust the door leaf

If the door opens on its own or won't latch properly — it may have warped or be incorrectly set. In most cases this can be resolved by adjusting the hinges, without replacement.

🍂 Seasonal
04

Treat seals with silicone spray

Once a year, treat door seals with silicone aerosol — they will stay flexible, won't crack and will provide better thermal insulation. This is cheaper than replacement.

🔄 Regular
05

Watch the gap at the threshold

If you can see a strip of light from outside under the door — you have a draught. Check the threshold seal and replace it if necessary.

🍂 Seasonal
06

Protect doors from moisture

Wooden doors are especially sensitive to moisture — they can swell. Repaint or re-varnish the wooden surface regularly, especially on the exterior. PVC doors should also be washed to prevent dirt build-up near hinges.

🍂 Seasonal

Note: if the door won't close even after adjusting the hinges — the threshold or frame structure may be damaged. In such cases a professional inspection is required.

Mosquito Net Care & Maintenance

Proper care ensures your net retains its shape, protection and appearance for years.

01

Wash the net every spring

Before the season, wash the mosquito net with warm water and a mild soapy solution. Use a soft sponge — do not scrub or scratch, as this damages the mesh fibres.

🍂 Seasonal
02

Remove frame nets for winter

Frame mosquito nets should be removed before winter — the weight of snow and frost can damage the profile and stretch the mesh. Store in a dry place away from direct sunlight.

🍂 Seasonal
03

Lubricate the roller net mechanism

The cassette mechanism of a roller net should be lubricated once a year with silicone aerosol. This ensures smooth rolling and prevents the cassette from jamming.

🔄 Regular
04

Check magnets in magnetic nets

If a magnetic net no longer closes tightly — the magnets may have shifted or weakened. Regularly check the fastening and re-attach with double-sided tape if needed.

🔄 Regular
05

Don't patch holes yourself

A small hole in the mesh quickly expands. If your net is damaged, it is better to replace the mesh fabric entirely — it is inexpensive, preserves the frame and provides complete protection.

ℹ️ Good to know
06

Store pleated nets correctly

Pleated mosquito nets should not be folded — store them flat or carefully rolled. Careless storage creates permanent creases that affect the net's function and appearance.

ℹ️ Good to know

Remember: a mosquito net that doesn't fit properly will let insects in around the edges. If your net was fitted elsewhere or your window dimensions have changed — contact us for precise measuring and replacement.
